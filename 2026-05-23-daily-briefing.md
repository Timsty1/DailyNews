## 2. The Weekend Story — Iran/Hormuz Still Stuck + The Memorial-Day Liquidity Trap

### Iran — progress signals, but the two hardest knots are untied

The wires moved toward "deal optimism" Friday, but reading the actual statements, **the gap is on the two issues that matter most for oil**:

- **Hormuz tolls (the oil-price variable)**: Secretary of State **Marco Rubio** said there were **"good signs"** an agreement is in sight — but warned any deal is **"unfeasible"** if Iran pursues permanent control of Strait shipping. His line was unambiguous: **"No one in the world is in favor of a tolling system… Under no circumstances can we live in a world where… you have to pay them a toll in order to go through the Straits of Hormuz."** Washington's and Tehran's definitions of "opening the strait" still diverge.
- **Enriched uranium (the escalation variable)**: Trump has pledged to **recover Iran's enriched-uranium stockpile**; Washington fears it is destined for a weapon. Tehran (Supreme Leader directive) wants reserves to **stay in-country**. Unresolved.
- **Mediation**: Pakistan army chief **Asim Munir** traveled to Tehran Thursday as part of ongoing US–Iran mediation.
- **Backdrop**: Trump earlier (May 11) called the ceasefire on **"massive life support"** after rejecting a Tehran proposal; Iran had floated a "Hormuz-first, nuclear-later" sequencing (Axios, late April) that the US has resisted.
- **Maritime reality**: Hormuz traffic still effectively zero; **~20,000 seafarers stranded on ~2,000 vessels** (IMO). Even a deal leaves a cargo backlog, damaged infrastructure, and mine-clearance — so a *price* normalization lags any *political* breakthrough.

**Net**: this is **"progress without resolution."** That is precisely the configuration that produces a two-sided Monday gap in HK — a constructive headline gaps oil down and HSI up; a breakdown on tolls/uranium gaps Brent to $115+ and HSI −2–3%.

### The Memorial-Day liquidity trap (the structural risk this specific weekend)

- **Mon May 25**: US markets **CLOSED** (Memorial Day). HK markets **OPEN**.
- **Implication**: If a bullish Iran headline hits over the weekend, HK rallies Monday but there is **no US cash market to confirm/extend** — the move can fade into Tuesday. - **Practical takeaway**: weekend overnight delta should be **more** hedged than a normal weekend, not less. The HSCEI Jul put-spread + small VXX/UVXY tail are the cheap insurance here. Do not lift hedges into a US-holiday Monday.
