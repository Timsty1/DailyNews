### What the reported 60-day MOU covers
- **Strait of Hormuz reopens with NO tolls**; Iran **clears the mines** it laid.
- **US lifts its port blockade** and **issues sanctions waivers** so Iran can sell oil freely.
- Iran commits to **never pursue nuclear weapons** and to **negotiate a suspension of uranium enrichment** (deferred to later talks).
- **60-day window, extendable** by mutual consent.

### What is still DISPUTED (the live risk)
| Open issue | State of play |
|---|---|
| **Enriched-uranium stockpile / enrichment** | **Deferred**; not in the preliminary MOU — talks run after a Hormuz deal |
| **Control / supervision of the strait** | **Genuine dispute** (Iranian "supervision" vs MOU framework) |
| **Sequencing of sanctions relief** | Disagreement |
| **"War-end first" vs "nuclear first"** | Iran wants permanent ceasefire before nuclear talks |

**Market translation**: oil has already moved on optimism, so a *signed* MOU mostly **confirms** the oil drop (toward $90–95) rather than being a fresh surprise — the asymmetric risk is a **breakdown over strait-control or uranium** that snaps Brent back toward $110+. Net: **the oil hedge bleeds in the base/bull case but is cheap tail insurance; size it as a thin tail, not a core position.**
