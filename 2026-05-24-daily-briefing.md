## 2. The Weekend Story — The Iran Deal Is "Largely Negotiated" (and What It Does NOT Cover)

This is the binary the book has been hedging for six weeks. On Saturday May 23, **Trump said the agreement to reopen the Strait of Hormuz is "largely negotiated" and "will be announced soon."** Reading the reported terms (Axios "what's inside the deal," CNBC, NPR, Fortune), the structure is a **phased MOU**, not a comprehensive settlement:

### What the deal reportedly DOES (phase one / 60-day window)

- **Strait of Hormuz reopens with NO tolls.** This is the key reversal — Iran had been working with Oman on a framework to formalize a permanent toll regime; Trump rejected it and the reported MOU drops tolls for the 60-day window.
- **Iran clears the mines** it deployed in the strait so vessels pass freely.
- **US lifts its blockade of Iranian ports** and **issues sanctions waivers** allowing Iran to sell oil freely.
- **Broader negotiations follow in 30–60 days.**

### What the deal does NOT cover (the live risk)

- **No agreement on the nuclear program.** Trump conspicuously did not mention nuclear terms.
- **Iran has NOT agreed to surrender its highly enriched uranium (HEU) stockpile** — the very issue Washington spent weeks calling non-negotiable. Tehran says the nuclear file is **not part of the preliminary agreement**; nuclear talks are expected to run **1–2 months after** any Hormuz deal.
- **Net**: the US has effectively accepted the **"Hormuz-first, nuclear-later"** sequencing it resisted through April (Axios first flagged this Iranian proposal April 27). That gets oil flowing and ships moving, but it **defers — not resolves — the escalation variable.**

**Practical takeaway**: the catastrophic oil-spike scenario that justified a full oil hedge is now the *low-probability* branch. But (1) the deal is **announced, not signed**; (2) HEU is unresolved and could unravel phase two; (3) **Memorial-Day Monday means HK reprices with no US confirmation.** So the move is: **let the oil hedge come off into a confirmed-deal pop rather than defend it, keep a thin uranium-failure tail, and do NOT chase a Monday gap-up in HK tech** — a no-US-tape Monday rally is the classic candidate to fade into Tuesday.
