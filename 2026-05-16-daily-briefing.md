## 2. TOP STORY — The Week That Cracked the Asia-Pacific AI Trade

### What actually happened, in three layered shocks

- **Boeing**: 200 jets confirmed, vs the 500 (plus widebody follow-on) telegraphed; BA shares −4% on Wall Street.
- **Tariffs / Section 301**: no roadmap to roll back; Trump's April baseline regime intact.
- **Soybeans / energy**: pledged, no dollar figures.
- **Taiwan**: Xi warned that mishandling "puts the relationship in great jeopardy."
- **AI chips**: US officially cleared H200 export licenses for Alibaba, Tencent, ByteDance, JD.com and 6 other Chinese firms (75,000 chips/firm max) — **but Beijing instructed buyers to defer; zero deliveries booked**.
- **Future**: Xi to visit US in fall on Trump's invitation.

Wall Street's read (Fortune, Euronews, NPR, Heritage): "no real substance." Boeing's −4% slide tells you the market was priced for 500 jets, not 200; the Asia tape took the same haircut on the broader theme. NBC and Maddow framed it as "few clear wins" / "empty-handed."
